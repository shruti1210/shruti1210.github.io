Bachelor Thesis

I worked at Festo AG & Co. KG, Esslingen, Germany with Dr Rainer Nitche in Robotic Systems Design from July 2019 till Dec 2019 on my undergraduate thesis. Initially I contributed towards a custom Festo library, aimed to facilitate Model-based design process simpler; but developing custom modules in C language integrated into this custom library for corresponding penumatic blocks. Eventually, I edited the joint model, to be used for further simulation. I worked towards mathematical understanding non-linear control theory. And further implemented a cascaded control, with a pressure controller inner loop and position control outer loop in Simulink. Later using dSpace hardware configuration, MicroAutoBox II implemented simulated pressure controller over a test bench.

Technologies Implemented: C, MATLAB, Simulink, dSpace, Latex




Control System Design

I worked at Control Systems Engineering Laboratory, Graduate School of Engineering, Hiroshima University, Japan, with Dr Shin Wakitani in control systems design. I worked there as an ILDP exchange student from May 2018 - July 2018. I was the only sophomore student nominated for the internship program with rest three of them being in their final year or pre-final years in about 4000 students in my university. I worked initially with mathematical modelling of linear and non-linear control systems. Later, worked on the system identification and control of a DC motor using HIL-simulation. I worked and compared PID, I-PD and fuzzy control approaches.

Technologies Implemented: MATLAB-Simulink, Hardware-In-Loop-Simulation




Dynamic Modelling and Control of a manipulator

I worked at CRIS Lab, BITS Pilani with Prof Bijoy Kumar Rout on development of a dynamic model of a 2-dof RR robot manipulator with joint flexibility. Flexible joints are modelled as equivalent to that of rigid links and torsional spring. A mapping between the joint variables and input torque is derived using symbolic mathematics in MATLAB, which is later integrated with a Simulink block to derive a PID controller.

Technologies Implemented: Mathematical Modeling, MATLAB, Simulink
